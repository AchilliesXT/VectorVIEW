Polar Pattern Theory

Understanding the mathematics of sensitivity.

What is a Polar Plot?

A polar plot is a graph used by engineers to visualize how sensitive a device (like a microphone or antenna) is to signals coming from different angles.


The distance from the center ($r$) represents sensitivity (gain). The angle ($\theta$) represents the direction of the incoming signal.

The General Equation

Most standard microphone patterns follow the "Pascal's Lima√ßon" family of curves:


$r = A + B \cos(\theta)$


  • If $A=1, B=0$: You get a Circle (Omnidirectional).
  • If $A=0.5, B=0.5$: You get a Heart (Cardioid).
  • If $A=0, B=1$: You get a Figure-8 (Bi-directional).
